Research

Four Pillars of
Computational Bioengineering

01

Metabolic Modeling

Genome-scale network reconstruction, kinetic and stoichiometric models of cellular metabolism. We develop Bayesian frameworks to sample feasible kinetic parameter spaces and predict metabolic fluxes from sparse experimental data.

Related Papers · GRASP · LooplessFluxSampler
02

Systems Biotechnology

Engineering biological systems for sustainable production of chemicals, fuels, and pharmaceuticals.

Related Papers
03

Bioprocess Optimization

Advanced optimization frameworks for bioreactor design, control, and scale-up strategies.

Related Papers
04

Machine Learning

Computational statistics and ML techniques to infer, predict, and design biological behavior from multi-omics data.

Related Papers

Open-Source Tools

Five software packages publicly released — METACONE, GRASP, LooplessFluxSampler, Fast-SNP, ll-ACHRB — with combined citations in 150+ works.

The People Behind the Science

PS

Prof. Pedro A. Saa

Principal Investigator

pnsaa@uc.cl

Faculty member at the Department of Chemical and Bioprocess Engineering and the Institute for Mathematical and Computational Engineering of PUC Chile. My group develops computational platforms at the intersection of optimization, Bayesian statistics, and machine learning to understand and engineer metabolism — from single-cell models to industrial bioprocesses.
